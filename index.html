BOEING ACHIEVES BREAKTHROUGH IN AI-NATIVE DEVELOPMENT

Aerospace & Defense Correspondent

In what industry observers are calling a remarkable demonstration of modern software delivery capabilities, Boeing Digital Services has achieved a transformative milestone in their engineering operations.

Working with Liatrio's specialized team, Boeing deployed Claude Sonnet and Opus models through Azure AI Foundry, implementing Spec-Driven Development across both greenfield and brownfield product teams. The flagship achievement: a legacy Azure provisioning workflow that previously required five business days and multiple team handoffs was rebuilt and deployed in under one week.

The final service now provisions complete Azure accounts in just fifteen minutes with zero handoffs—a 96% reduction in processing time. A multi-day Flashbuild event spanning two product teams and a platform team delivered production features in just two days, entirely powered by Claude models and Liatrio's SDD framework.

HEALTHCARE GIANT SCALES AI ACROSS 80+ ENGINEERING TEAMS

Healthcare & Life Sciences Reporter

Natera's Quality Control and Intelligence (QCI) engineering team has become the epicenter of an organic AI adoption revolution that promises to transform genomics and diagnostics development at enterprise scale.

Through Liatrio's embedded partnership, Claude Code was integrated directly into the team's development environment with comprehensive OpenTelemetry instrumentation, providing leadership unprecedented real-time visibility into AI adoption patterns and productivity metrics.

The deployment encompasses Spec-Driven Development workflows and automated merge request review pipelines across core regulated repositories—a significant achievement in the highly controlled FDA-regulated software environment.

Perhaps most notably, adoption has become organic and self-sustaining. Engineers are independently extending Claude usage beyond Liatrio's initial recommendations: one engineer executed a complete 14-step clinical workflow dry-test without prompting, while another extended the MR review pipeline to her own healthcare life sciences project within days of the initial training.

"We didn't write a single line of code" has become a frequent refrain among engineers describing certain development tasks, indicating the profound impact of AI-assisted development on traditional software engineering practices.
