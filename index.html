AZURE PROVISIONING TIME PLUMMETS FROM FIVE BUSINESS DAYS TO FIFTEEN MINUTES
Boeing Digital Services engineers stunned as Claude-powered workflow eliminates every manual handoff in under a week
By the Liatrio Data Desk  ·  Enterprise Aerospace & Defense

In what industry analysts are calling a landmark moment for enterprise AI adoption, Boeing Digital Services has completed a sweeping transformation of its Azure account provisioning workflow — reducing a process that previously consumed five full business days and required handoffs across multiple teams down to a brisk fifteen minutes with zero human intervention required.

The transformation was orchestrated by Liatrio, using Claude Sonnet and Opus through Azure AI Foundry, paired with the firm's proprietary Spec-Driven Development framework. The flagship result: a legacy provisioning workflow rebuilt in under a week now spins up a complete Azure account in 15 minutes with no handoffs.

The efficiency gains are staggering. Liatrio estimates delivery is now 8× faster than comparable work without AI tooling — the equivalent of compressing two-plus months of engineering effort into under a week. Three product teams and one platform team participated in a multi-day "Flashbuild" powered entirely by Claude models, delivering production features in just two days.

Sources close to the project report that Claude Code approval is currently in progress as the team's preferred development harness for the second half of 2026, signaling Boeing's intent to deepen its commitment to the Claude ecosystem across its engineering organization.

NATERA ENGINEERS DECLARE: "I DIDN'T WRITE A SINGLE LINE OF CODE"
FDA-regulated clinical software teams embrace Claude at unprecedented pace; one engineer runs 14-step workflow unprompted
By the Liatrio Health Sciences Correspondent  ·  Healthcare & Life Sciences

In the closely regulated halls of Natera's genomics engineering division, something remarkable is happening: engineers are reaching for Claude before they reach for their keyboards. Liatrio embedded Claude Code directly into Natera's QCI engineering team with full OpenTelemetry instrumentation, giving leadership real-time visibility into AI adoption across four core regulated repositories.

What surprised even Liatrio's deployment team was the speed of organic uptake. Engineers began self-directing Claude usage far beyond prescribed workflows. One engineer ran a fourteen-step clinical workflow dry-test entirely unprompted. Another extended Liatrio's automated MR review pipeline to her own HCLS project within days of first seeing it demonstrated.

Liatrio has designed a five-level AI Fluency Framework — L1 through L5 — to track and structure this progression from awareness to expert-level autonomous deployment. The framework is now being adopted as a company-wide standard.

The expansion roadmap is ambitious: from a single QCI team to more than 80 engineering teams spanning all of Natera's genomics and diagnostics divisions. Five major plays are in development, including AI-accelerated FDA and MasterControl compliance workflows and an agentic SDLC quality orchestration layer across the entire CI/CD stack.

LIATRIO BUILDS ENTERPRISE AI CERTIFICATION PROGRAM IN 72 HOURS; EXPERTS BAFFLED
The Claude Certification Academy goes from blank document to full curriculum over a long weekend — 50+ engineers upskilling within the first month
By the Liatrio Workforce Development Desk  ·  The Enablement Method

When demand for Anthropic Claude professional services began outpacing available talent earlier this year, most firms scheduled a planning retreat. Liatrio built a certification program. In three days.

The Claude Certification Academy — known internally as the CCA — takes engineers from zero familiarity with AI-assisted development to productive, certified practitioners in days rather than months. Built atop Anthropic's open-source learning materials and tailored to the pressures of enterprise delivery, the curriculum has become Liatrio's primary internal training vehicle, administered through its Forge cohort program.

Within the first month of the Forge program going live, more than 50 engineers were actively upskilling on the platform — a figure the firm expects to grow substantially as client engagements multiply.

Liatrio's leadership is direct about its differentiation: "Other partners will stand up Bedrock," one executive told this correspondent. "We teach organizations how to actually transform. That's the flywheel — certified engineers who go into Boeing, Natera, and every client after them, and change how engineering work gets done." The CCA is, in short, the mechanism by which Liatrio scales itself.
